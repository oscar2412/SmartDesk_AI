# 1. Password Reset

Your Roadmap Consulting password protects access to your computer, email, VPN, business applications, and company data. Never share your password with anyone, including coworkers, managers, vendors, or IT staff.

---

## 1.1 How to Reset a Forgotten Password

Use the Self-Service Password Portal when you forget your password.

**Self-Service Password Portal:**
[https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com](https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com)

### Steps

1. Open a web browser from your computer, phone, or tablet.
2. Go to [https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com](https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com).
3. Select **Forgot Password** or **Reset Password**.
4. Enter your Roadmap Consulting username or work email address.
5. Complete the identity verification steps.

   * You may be asked to verify using MFA.
   * You may receive a verification code.
   * You may need to answer security questions if configured.
6. Enter a new password that meets Roadmap Consulting password requirements.
7. Confirm the new password.
8. Submit the change.
9. Wait for confirmation that the password reset was successful.
10. Sign in again using your new password.

### After Resetting Your Password

After a password reset, update the password anywhere it may be saved.

Check the following:

* Windows or Mac sign-in.
* Mobile email app.
* VPN client.
* Microsoft Teams or other collaboration tools.
* Browser-saved passwords.
* Mapped drives or internal applications.
* Any application that uses your Roadmap Consulting account.

### Important Note

If a device keeps trying to sign in with your old password, your account may lock again. Update saved passwords promptly.

---

## 1.2 How to Unlock a Locked Account

Your account may lock after several unsuccessful sign-in attempts. This protects your account from unauthorized access.

### Steps

1. Open a web browser.
2. Go to [https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com](https://passwords.roadmapconsulting.com).
3. Select **Unlock Account**.
4. Enter your Roadmap Consulting username or work email address.
5. Complete the identity verification steps.
6. Wait for confirmation that the account was unlocked.
7. Sign in again using your current password.
8. If the account locks again, stop trying and contact IT Support.

### Common Reasons an Account Locks

Your account may lock because:

* You entered the wrong password too many times.
* Your phone is trying to sync email using an old password.
* Your VPN client has an outdated saved password.
* Outlook, Teams, or another application is using an old password.
* A browser has an outdated saved password.
* You recently changed your password but did not update it on all devices.

### What to Do If the Account Keeps Locking

1. Stop repeated sign-in attempts.
2. Check your mobile phone email settings.
3. Check your VPN saved credentials.
4. Restart your computer.
5. Contact IT Support:

   * Email: [it-support@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:it-support@roadmapconsulting.com)
   * Phone: ext. 2026
   * Portal: [https://it.roadmapconsulting.com](https://it.roadmapconsulting.com)

## 1.3 Password Complexity Requirements

Your password must meet Roadmap Consulting security standards.

### Password Requirements

Your password must:

* Be at least **12 characters long**.
* Include at least **one uppercase letter**.
* Include at least **one lowercase letter**.
* Include at least **one number**.
* Include at least **one special character**, such as `!`, `@`, `#`, `$`, `%`, `&`, or `*`.
* Not contain your name.
* Not contain your username.
* Not contain the company name.
* Not be a commonly used password.
* Not reuse recent passwords.
* Not be shared with anyone.

### Recommended Password Approach

Use a passphrase that is easy for you to remember but hard for someone else to guess.

Example format:

`Coffee!Trail72Window`

Do not use this exact example. Create your own unique password.

### Avoid These Password Choices

Do not use:

* `Password123!`
* `Roadmap2026!`
* Your name plus a number.
* Your child’s name.
* Your pet’s name.
* Birthdays or anniversary dates.
* Sports teams followed by a number.
* Anything you have used on a personal account.

# 2. VPN Setup

The Roadmap Consulting VPN allows employees to securely access internal company systems when working remotely or outside the office network.

Use VPN only for approved business purposes.

---

## 2.1 When VPN Is Required

You may need VPN to access:

* Internal business applications.
* File shares.
* Administrative systems.
* Development or test systems.
* Internal reporting tools.
* Systems restricted to the company network.

You may not need VPN for cloud-based applications such as email, Teams, or web-based services unless IT specifically instructs you to connect.

---

## 2.2 Step-by-Step VPN Installation on Windows 11

Use these steps to install the approved Roadmap Consulting VPN client on a Windows 11 computer.

### Before You Begin

Make sure you have:

* A working internet connection.
* Your Roadmap Consulting username and password.
* Access to MFA.
* Local administrator permissions, if required.
* Time to restart your computer if prompted.

### Installation Steps

3. Open Microsoft Edge, Chrome, or another approved browser.

5. Search for **VPN Client for Windows 11**.
6. Open the VPN installation article or request page.
7. Download the approved Windows VPN installer.
8. Go to your **Downloads** folder.
9. Double-click the VPN installer file.
10. If Windows asks for permission, select **Yes**.
11. Follow the installation prompts.
12. Accept the license agreement if prompted.
13. Keep the default installation settings unless IT provided different instructions.
14. Complete the installation.
15. Restart your computer if prompted.
16. Open the VPN client from the Start menu.
17. Add or select the Roadmap Consulting VPN profile.
18. Enter the VPN server address listed in the IT Portal instructions.
19. Save the profile.
20. Sign in using your Roadmap Consulting username and password.
21. Complete MFA when prompted.
22. Confirm the VPN status shows **Connected**.

# 3. Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-Factor Authentication, commonly called MFA, protects your account by requiring an additional verification step beyond your password.

---

## 3.1 What MFA Is and Why It Is Required

MFA requires two or more ways to verify your identity.

### Common MFA Factors

MFA may use:

* Something you know, such as your password.
* Something you have, such as your smartphone.
* Something you are, such as fingerprint or face recognition, if supported.

### Why MFA Is Required

MFA is required because:

* Passwords can be stolen or guessed.
* MFA helps prevent unauthorized account access.
* MFA protects company email and data.
* MFA reduces the risk of phishing attacks.
* MFA helps Roadmap Consulting meet security and compliance expectations.

### Important MFA Safety Rule

Never approve an MFA request you did not initiate. If you receive an unexpected MFA prompt, deny it and report it to IT Support.

---

## 3.2 How to Set Up MFA on a Smartphone

Use this process when registering a phone for MFA.

### Steps

1. On your computer, sign in to your Roadmap Consulting account.
2. When prompted to set up MFA, select **Begin Setup** or **Next**.
3. Choose **Mobile Phone** or **Smartphone**.
4. Enter your mobile phone number if prompted.
5. Select your preferred verification method:

   * Text message code.
   * Phone call.
   * Push notification through an authenticator app.
6. Follow the on-screen instructions.
7. If you receive a text message code, enter the code on your computer.
8. If you receive a phone call, answer the call and follow the instructions.
9. If you receive a push notification, approve the prompt only if you initiated the sign-in.
10. Confirm that MFA setup completed successfully.
11. Sign out and sign back in to test MFA.

---

## 3.3 How to Set Up MFA Using an Authenticator App

An authenticator app is the preferred MFA method because it is secure, reliable, and easy to use.

### Before You Begin

Make sure you have:

* Your smartphone.
* The approved authenticator app installed.
* Your Roadmap Consulting username and password.
* Internet access on your phone or computer.

### Steps

1. Install the approved authenticator app on your smartphone.
2. On your computer, sign in to your Roadmap Consulting account.
3. When prompted for MFA setup, select **Authenticator App**.
4. Open the authenticator app on your smartphone.
5. Tap **Add Account**.
6. Select **Work or School Account** if prompted.
7. Use your phone camera to scan the QR code displayed on your computer.
8. If you cannot scan the QR code, choose the manual setup option and enter the setup code.
9. Wait for the account to appear in the authenticator app.
10. On your computer, click **Next**.
11. Approve the test notification or enter the code shown in the app.
12. Confirm that setup was successful.
13. Keep the authenticator app installed on your phone.
14. Do not delete the Roadmap Consulting account from the app unless IT instructs you to do so.

---

## 3.4 What to Do If You Lose Access to Your MFA Device

If your phone is lost, stolen, replaced, wiped, or damaged, you may not be able to sign in.

### Steps

1. Do not repeatedly attempt to sign in.
2. Contact IT Support immediately:

   * Email: [it-support@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:it-support@roadmapconsulting.com)
   * Phone: ext. 2026
   * Portal: [https://it.roadmapconsulting.com](https://it.roadmapconsulting.com)
3. Tell IT that you lost access to your MFA device.
4. Be prepared to verify your identity.
5. IT will reset or re-register your MFA methods.
6. After IT resets MFA, set it up again using your new or available device.
7. Test your sign-in before ending the support session.

### If Your Phone Was Lost or Stolen

1. Report the lost or stolen device to IT immediately.
2. Tell IT whether company email was configured on the device.
3. IT may remove company access from the device.
4. Change your password if IT instructs you to do so.
5. Re-register MFA on your replacement device.

2. Restart your laptop.
3. Close applications you are not using.
4. Close unnecessary browser tabs.
5. Confirm your laptop is plugged into power.
6. Confirm your internet connection is stable.
7. Check whether the issue happens only in one application.
8. Check whether other employees are experiencing the same issue.
9. Install approved updates if prompted by IT.
10. Submit a ticket if the laptop remains slow.

---

### Windows 11 Performance Checks

1. Press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc** to open Task Manager.
2. Click **Processes**.
3. Review CPU, Memory, and Disk usage.
4. Look for applications using unusually high resources.
5. Do not end processes unless you know what they are.
6. Restart the laptop if usage remains high.
7. Take a screenshot if the issue continues.
8. Attach the screenshot to your IT ticket.

---

### Mac Performance Checks

1. Open **Activity Monitor**.
2. Review CPU and Memory usage.
3. Look for applications using unusually high resources.
4. Close unused applications.
5. Restart your Mac.
6. Check available storage.
7. Submit a ticket if performance does not improve.

---

### What to Include in a Slow Laptop Ticket

Include:

* Laptop model.
* Whether the issue started today or has been ongoing.
* Whether the laptop is slow all the time or only with certain applications.
* Whether you are remote or in the office.
* Whether you recently installed software.
* Screenshots of errors or high resource usage.
* Any troubleshooting steps already completed.

* Power adapter is damaged.
* Docking station is not working.

9. Explain whether the issue prevents you from working.
10. State whether you need a loaner device.

## 7.3 Loaner Device Request Process

Loaner devices may be available when your assigned device is being repaired, replaced, or unavailable.

### When to Request a Loaner Device

Request a loaner if:

* Your laptop will not power on.
* Your assigned laptop is being repaired.
* Your laptop was lost or stolen.
* Your laptop has damage that prevents you from working.
* You are waiting for a replacement device.
* A business-critical issue requires temporary equipment.

### Steps

1. Submit a hardware issue ticket at [https://it.roadmapconsulting.com](https://it.roadmapconsulting.com).
2. In the ticket, state that you need a loaner device.
3. Explain the business impact.
4. Include your department.
5. Include your location.
6. Include your availability for pickup or delivery.
7. Wait for IT to confirm whether a loaner is available.
8. Pick up the loaner or follow shipping instructions if remote.
9. Sign any required equipment acknowledgment form.
10. Sign in and confirm you can access required applications.
11. Save work files only to approved company storage.
12. Return the loaner when your assigned device is repaired or replaced.

### Loaner Device Expectations

Employees using loaner devices must:

* Treat the loaner as company property.
* Use it only for business purposes.
* Avoid installing unapproved software.
* Save files to approved company storage.
* Report any issues with the loaner immediately.
* Return the laptop, charger, and accessories.
* Remove personal files before returning it, if any were temporarily stored.
