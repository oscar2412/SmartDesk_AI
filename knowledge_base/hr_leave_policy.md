## Important Policy Note

This policy describes Roadmap Consulting’s internal leave benefits and procedures. It should be read together with applicable federal, state, and local employment laws.

Roadmap Consulting is based in Texas. Texas generally does not require private employers to provide vacation, sick, or parental leave unless required under another law or provided by the employer’s own policy. However, once an employer creates a written leave policy, the company should follow that policy consistently. ([Texas Guidebook][1])

Eligible employees may also qualify for job-protected leave under the federal Family and Medical Leave Act, commonly called FMLA. Under the FMLA, eligible employees generally must have worked for a covered employer for at least 12 months, worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12 months before leave begins, and work at a location where the employer has at least 50 employees within 75 miles. ([DOL][2])

For questions about legal eligibility, special medical situations, pregnancy-related needs, disability accommodation, or extended leave, contact HR at **[hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com)** or **ext. 3030**.

# 1. Casual Leave

Casual Leave is intended for short, personal, non-medical absences that cannot always be scheduled far in advance. Examples include personal appointments, family needs, school matters, urgent errands, personal commitments, or other brief absences.

---

## 1.1 Casual Leave Entitlement

Eligible full-time employees are entitled to:

* **8 Casual Leave days per calendar year**
* Casual Leave may be taken in:

  * Full-day increments
  * Half-day increments
  * Hourly increments, if approved by the manager and supported by the Leave Management System

Part-time employees may receive Casual Leave on a prorated basis based on their regular work schedule.

---

## 1.2 How to Apply for Casual Leave

Employees should apply for Casual Leave through the Leave Management System.

**Leave Management System:**
[https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com)

### Steps

1. Log in to [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
2. Select **Apply for Leave**.
3. Choose **Casual Leave** as the leave type.
4. Enter the requested date or date range.
5. Select whether the leave is for:

   * Full day
   * Half day
   * Hourly leave, if available
6. Enter a brief reason for the request.
7. Confirm your available Casual Leave balance.
8. Submit the request.
9. Wait for manager approval before finalizing personal plans, unless the leave is urgent.
10. Check the Leave Management System for approval status.

---

## 1.3 Notice Period Required for Casual Leave

Employees should provide as much advance notice as possible.

### Standard Notice Requirements

* Planned Casual Leave: **At least 2 business days in advance**
* Half-day Casual Leave: **At least 1 business day in advance**
* Urgent Casual Leave: Notify your manager as soon as possible
* Same-day Casual Leave: Allowed only for urgent or unexpected personal matters

### Emergency Situations

If the need is urgent and you cannot submit the request first:

1. Notify your manager by phone, Teams, text, or email as soon as possible.
2. Contact HR if you cannot reach your manager.
3. Submit the leave request in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com) as soon as you are able.
4. Add a note explaining that the request was due to an urgent situation.

---

## 1.4 Approval Process and Who Approves Casual Leave

Casual Leave must be approved by the employee’s direct manager.

### Approval Process

1. Employee submits the request in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
2. The direct manager receives the request.
3. The manager reviews:

   * Team coverage
   * Business priorities
   * Client commitments
   * Employee leave balance
   * Timing of the request
4. The manager approves, rejects, or asks for more information.
5. The employee receives the decision through the Leave Management System.
6. HR may review requests if there is a policy question, repeated issue, or dispute.

### Approval Authority

* Primary approver: **Direct Manager**
* Secondary approver, if manager is unavailable: **Department Head**
* Policy or balance questions: **HR**
* HR contact: **[hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com)** or **ext. 3030**

---

## 1.5 Carry Forward Rules for Casual Leave

Casual Leave is intended to be used during the calendar year.

### Carry Forward Rules

* Unused Casual Leave **does not carry forward** to the next calendar year.
* Unused Casual Leave is **not paid out** at year-end.
* Casual Leave cannot be converted into Earned Leave.
* Casual Leave cannot be donated or transferred to another employee.

### Year-End Reminder

Employees are encouraged to monitor their leave balance regularly in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com) and plan responsibly.

# 2. Sick Leave

Sick Leave is provided so employees can take time away from work when they are ill, injured, attending medical appointments, recovering from medical treatment, or caring for an immediate family member with a health-related need.

---

## 2.1 Sick Leave Entitlement

Eligible full-time employees are entitled to:

* **10 Sick Leave days per calendar year**

Part-time employees may receive Sick Leave on a prorated basis based on their regular work schedule.

Sick Leave may be used for:

* Personal illness
* Injury
* Medical appointments
* Recovery from medical treatment
* Mental health needs
* Care for an immediate family member
* Medical testing or follow-up care

---

## 2.2 How to Report a Sick Day

Employees should notify their manager as soon as they know they are unable to work.

### Steps

1. Notify your direct manager before your scheduled start time, when possible.
2. Use the manager’s preferred communication method, such as phone, Teams, text, or email.
3. State that you are unable to work due to illness or medical reasons.
4. Provide the expected duration of your absence, if known.
5. Do not provide private medical details unless you choose to or HR specifically requests documentation.
6. Submit the Sick Leave request in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com) as soon as you are able.
7. If you are unable to submit the request, contact HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com) or ext. 3030.
8. Keep your manager updated if your expected return date changes.

---

## 2.3 Medical Certificate Requirements

Roadmap Consulting may request medical documentation in certain situations.

### Medical Documentation May Be Required When

* Sick Leave exceeds **3 consecutive workdays**
* There is a repeated pattern of unscheduled absences
* The employee requests extended medical leave
* The employee is returning from a serious illness, injury, or medical procedure
* HR needs documentation to assess FMLA, disability accommodation, or long-term leave eligibility

### Documentation Guidelines

Medical documentation should generally confirm:

* That the employee was unable to work
* The dates covered by the medical absence
* Any work restrictions, if applicable
* Expected return-to-work date, if known

Employees should not submit unnecessary private medical details. HR will handle medical documentation confidentially and limit access to those with a business need to know.

---

## 2.4 What Happens If Sick Leave Is Exhausted

If an employee uses all available Sick Leave, HR will review available options.

### Possible Options

Depending on the situation, the employee may be able to use:

* Earned Leave
* Casual Leave, if appropriate
* Unpaid leave
* FMLA leave, if eligible
* Short-term disability benefits, if available
* Reasonable accommodation, if applicable
* Modified work schedule, if approved

### Steps

1. Contact HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com) before your Sick Leave balance reaches zero, if possible.
2. Explain the expected duration of the absence.
3. Submit any required documentation.
4. HR will review available leave balances and eligibility.
5. HR will coordinate with your manager regarding work coverage.
6. HR will confirm next steps in writing.

---

## 2.5 Long-Term Illness Procedure

A long-term illness is generally an illness, injury, or medical condition expected to keep an employee away from work for more than **5 consecutive workdays** or require ongoing intermittent leave.

### Steps

1. Notify your manager as soon as you know the absence may extend beyond 5 workdays.
2. Contact HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com) or ext. 3030.
3. Submit a Sick Leave request in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
4. HR may provide additional forms for medical leave review.
5. Submit medical documentation by the requested deadline.
6. HR will review whether FMLA, disability accommodation, unpaid leave, or other benefits may apply.
7. HR and your manager will discuss work coverage while respecting your privacy.
8. Provide updated documentation if your return date changes.
9. Before returning, submit a fitness-for-duty or return-to-work note if HR requests one.
10. HR will confirm your approved return date, restrictions, and work schedule.

### FMLA Note

Roadmap Consulting has 105 employees, so eligible employees may qualify for FMLA protection depending on their length of service, hours worked, and worksite eligibility. FMLA generally provides eligible employees up to 12 workweeks of unpaid, job-protected leave in a 12-month period for qualifying reasons. ([DOL][3])

# 3. Earned Leave

Earned Leave is planned paid time off that employees may use for vacation, rest, personal commitments, travel, family time, or other planned absences.

---

## 3.1 Earned Leave Entitlement

Eligible full-time employees receive:

* **15 Earned Leave days per calendar year**

Part-time employees may receive Earned Leave on a prorated basis based on their regular work schedule.

Earned Leave is separate from Casual Leave and Sick Leave.

---

## 3.2 How Earned Leave Is Accrued Month by Month

Earned Leave accrues gradually throughout the year.

### Monthly Accrual

Full-time employees accrue:

* **1.25 Earned Leave days per month**
* Total annual accrual: **15 days per year**

### Accrual Rules

* Earned Leave begins accruing from the employee’s date of hire.
* New hires accrue leave on a prorated basis for their first month, depending on the start date.
* Employees must be actively employed to accrue leave.
* Leave does not accrue during extended unpaid leave unless required by law or approved by HR.
* Leave balances are visible in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).

### Example

If a full-time employee works the entire year:

| Month     | Monthly Accrual | Running Balance |
| --------- | --------------: | --------------: |
| January   |       1.25 days |       1.25 days |
| February  |       1.25 days |       2.50 days |
| March     |       1.25 days |       3.75 days |
| April     |       1.25 days |       5.00 days |
| May       |       1.25 days |       6.25 days |
| June      |       1.25 days |       7.50 days |
| July      |       1.25 days |       8.75 days |
| August    |       1.25 days |      10.00 days |
| September |       1.25 days |      11.25 days |
| October   |       1.25 days |      12.50 days |
| November  |       1.25 days |      13.75 days |
| December  |       1.25 days |      15.00 days |

---

## 3.3 How to Apply for Earned Leave

Earned Leave should be planned in advance whenever possible.

### Steps

1. Review your project deadlines, customer commitments, and team calendar.
2. Check your available Earned Leave balance at [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
3. Discuss timing with your manager if the leave is longer than 3 workdays.
4. Log in to [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
5. Select **Apply for Leave**.
6. Choose **Earned Leave**.
7. Enter the leave start date and end date.
8. Confirm whether the request includes full days, half days, or approved partial days.
9. Add a brief note, such as “planned vacation” or “personal time off.”
10. Submit the request.
11. Wait for manager approval before booking non-refundable travel.
12. Once approved, coordinate handoff of work before your leave begins.

---

## 3.4 Carry Forward Rules and Limits

Employees may carry forward a limited amount of unused Earned Leave.

### Carry Forward Rules

* Employees may carry forward up to **10 unused Earned Leave days** into the next calendar year.
* Any unused Earned Leave above 10 days at year-end may be forfeited unless HR approves an exception.
* The maximum Earned Leave balance at any time is **25 days**.
* Once an employee reaches the maximum balance, additional accrual may pause until the balance drops below the cap.
* Managers should encourage employees to use Earned Leave regularly to support rest, recovery, and sustainable performance.

### Exception Requests

Employees may request an exception if business needs prevented them from taking leave.

#### Steps

1. Submit a written request to HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com).
2. Include the reason leave could not be used.
3. Include the number of days requested for exception.
4. Include manager confirmation, if available.
5. HR will review and respond in writing.

---

## 3.5 Leave Encashment Policy

Leave encashment means payment for unused Earned Leave instead of taking time off.

### Roadmap Consulting Encashment Rules

* Earned Leave is primarily intended to be used as time off.
* Leave encashment is not automatic.
* Encashment may be allowed only when approved by HR and Finance.
* Casual Leave and Sick Leave are not eligible for encashment.
* Encashment may be considered:

  * At separation from employment, if allowed by written policy
  * During a company-approved encashment window
  * In special circumstances approved by HR and Finance

Texas guidance states that payment for accrued leave is generally required only if the employer has promised such payment in a written policy or agreement. Therefore, Roadmap Consulting’s written policy controls whether unused leave is payable. ([Texas Guidebook][4])

### Separation from Employment

Upon separation, Roadmap Consulting will review the employee’s final Earned Leave balance and determine payout eligibility based on:

* This written policy
* Employment agreement, if applicable
* Final pay rules
* Any applicable legal requirements
* HR and Finance review

### Encashment Request Steps

1. Log in to [https://hr.roadmapconsulting.com](https://hr.roadmapconsulting.com).
2. Open an HR request.
3. Select **Leave Encashment Inquiry** or the closest available category.
4. Enter your current Earned Leave balance.
5. State the reason for the request.
6. Submit the request.
7. HR will coordinate with Finance.
8. HR will confirm approval or denial in writing.

# 4. Work From Home Policy

Roadmap Consulting supports flexible work where business needs, role requirements, performance expectations, customer commitments, and security requirements can be met.

Work from home, also called WFH, is a work arrangement. It is not a leave type. Employees working from home are expected to be available, responsive, productive, and working during approved work hours.

---

## 4.1 Who Is Eligible for Work From Home

Eligibility depends on role, performance, work responsibilities, security needs, customer commitments, and manager approval.

### Employees May Be Eligible If

* Their job duties can be performed remotely.
* They have reliable internet service.
* They have an appropriate work environment.
* Their role does not require full-time office presence.
* Their performance is meeting expectations.
* They can attend meetings and respond during working hours.
* They can protect company and customer information.

### Employees May Not Be Eligible If

* Their role requires physical presence in the office.
* They handle equipment, facilities, or in-person support duties.
* Performance or attendance concerns exist.
* The home environment is not suitable for confidential work.
* Internet service is unreliable.
* Customer or project needs require office presence.

---

## 4.2 Maximum Work From Home Days Per Week

The standard maximum WFH allowance is:

* **Up to 2 days per week**, with manager approval

Exceptions may be approved by department leadership and HR.

### Important Rules

* WFH days are not guaranteed.
* WFH may be modified based on business needs.
* WFH may be suspended during critical projects, customer events, audits, onboarding, training, or performance concerns.
* Employees must be available to come into the office when required.

---

## 4.3 How to Request a Work-From-Home Day

Employees should request WFH in advance unless there is an urgent situation.

### Steps

1. Review your calendar and work responsibilities.
2. Confirm that no in-person meeting, customer event, or office duty requires your presence.
3. Discuss recurring WFH needs with your manager.
4. Log in to [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com) or the HR Portal if WFH requests are managed there.
5. Select **Work From Home Request**.
6. Enter the requested WFH date.
7. Enter the reason, if required.
8. Confirm your work location and contact number.
9. Confirm you have reliable internet and required equipment.
10. Submit the request.
11. Wait for manager approval.
12. Once approved, update your calendar or status if required by your department.

---

## 4.4 Equipment and Internet Requirements

Employees working from home must have the tools needed to work securely and effectively.

### Required Equipment

Employees must use:

* Company-issued laptop, unless otherwise approved
* Approved headset or microphone for calls
* Webcam for meetings, if required
* Approved software and security tools
* VPN when required
* Company-approved storage locations for files

### Internet Requirements

Employees must have:

* Reliable high-speed internet
* Secure home Wi-Fi
* Password-protected router
* Stable connection for video meetings
* Ability to access company systems

### Security Requirements

Employees must:

* Lock the computer when stepping away.
* Keep company equipment secure.
* Avoid working where confidential information can be seen or overheard.
* Use VPN when required.
* Store files only in approved company systems.
* Not allow family members, visitors, or others to use company equipment.
* Report lost or stolen equipment immediately to IT and HR.

---

## 4.5 Performance Expectations While Working From Home

WFH employees are expected to meet the same performance standards as employees working in the office.

### Expectations

Employees must:

* Be available during scheduled work hours.
* Attend meetings on time.
* Respond to messages, calls, and emails promptly.
* Meet deadlines and service commitments.
* Keep calendars current.
* Maintain professional communication.
* Protect company and customer data.
* Follow all company policies.
* Notify the manager immediately if internet, power, or equipment issues prevent work.

### Communication Standards

While working from home:

1. Set your Teams or chat status accurately.
2. Attend scheduled meetings.
3. Use video when requested or appropriate.
4. Respond to urgent messages promptly.
5. Inform your manager if you step away for an extended period.
6. Keep customer and internal commitments visible.

---

## 4.6 Who Approves Work-From-Home Requests

WFH requests are approved by the employee’s direct manager.

### Approval Authority

* Primary approver: **Direct Manager**
* Exception approver: **Department Head**
* Policy review: **HR**
* Security or equipment review: **IT**, if needed

### Approval Considerations

Managers may consider:

* Business needs
* Customer commitments
* Team coverage
* Role requirements
* Employee performance
* Security requirements
* Frequency of WFH requests
* Prior attendance or communication concerns

# 5. Maternity and Paternity Leave

Roadmap Consulting provides parental leave benefits to support employees welcoming a child through birth, adoption, foster placement, or legal guardianship.

This section works alongside any applicable FMLA rights, state law, benefit plans, and company policies.

---

## 5.1 Maternity Leave Entitlement and Duration

Eligible employees giving birth are entitled to:

* **12 weeks of maternity leave**

This may include:

* Medical recovery following childbirth
* Bonding time with the child
* Time covered by FMLA, if eligible
* Use of available paid leave balances
* Any approved company-paid parental leave benefit

### Paid Portion

Roadmap Consulting provides:

* **6 weeks of paid maternity leave**
* Remaining approved time may be covered by:

  * Earned Leave
  * Sick Leave, if medically appropriate
  * Unpaid leave
  * Short-term disability benefits, if available
  * FMLA, if eligible

---

## 5.2 Paternity Leave Entitlement and Duration

Eligible employees who are non-birthing parents are entitled to:

* **4 weeks of paternity leave**

This may be used for:

* Bonding with a newborn
* Supporting the birthing parent
* Adoption placement
* Foster placement
* Legal guardianship transition

### Paid Portion

Roadmap Consulting provides:

* **2 weeks of paid paternity leave**
* Remaining approved time may be covered by:

  * Earned Leave
  * Unpaid leave
  * FMLA, if eligible

---

## 5.3 How to Apply and How Far in Advance

Employees should provide advance notice whenever the need for parental leave is foreseeable.

### Recommended Notice

* Expected birth, adoption, or placement: **At least 30 days in advance**
* Earlier notice is encouraged when possible
* Emergency or early delivery: Notify HR and your manager as soon as practical

### Steps

1. Notify your direct manager that you expect to request parental leave.
2. Contact HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com) or ext. 3030.
3. Log in to [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com).
4. Select the appropriate leave type:

   * Maternity Leave

6. Enter the expected return date.
7. Upload required documentation if requested by HR.

9. Submit the request.
10. HR will review eligibility, pay status, benefits, and documentation.
11. HR will confirm approval and next steps in writing.

Pay during maternity or paternity leave depends on the type of leave, available balances, and eligibility.

* Company-paid maternity leave for eligible employees

Paternity leave may include:

Roadmap Consulting wants employees to return successfully and smoothly after parental leave.

### Steps

2. Confirm your return-to-work date.
3. Notify your manager of your expected return.

   * Sick Leave
   * Earned Leave
   * Maternity Leave
   * Paternity Leave
   * Bereavement Leave
   * Public Holiday alternate day
   * Work From Home request, if managed in the system
8. Enter the start date and end date.
9. Select full day, half day, or partial day if available.
10. Enter a brief reason or note.
11. Upload required documentation, if applicable.
12. Review the request for accuracy.
13. Submit the request.
14. Monitor the request status.
15. Wait for approval before finalizing plans, unless the leave is an emergency or illness.
16. Coordinate work handoff before the leave begins.

## 8.3 What Happens If Leave Is Rejected

Leave may be rejected if there are business conflicts, insufficient leave balance, missing documentation, timing concerns, or team coverage issues.

### Common Reasons Leave May Be Rejected

* Insufficient leave balance
* Too many team members already out
* Critical project deadline
* Customer commitment
* Missing documentation
* Request submitted too late
* Incorrect leave type selected
* Performance or attendance concern related to WFH requests

### Steps If Your Leave Is Rejected

1. Review the reason provided in the Leave Management System.
2. Speak with your manager to understand the business concern.
3. Ask whether alternate dates are available.
4. Update and resubmit the request if appropriate.
5. Contact HR if you believe the rejection was inconsistent with policy.
6. HR will review the situation and provide guidance if needed.

### Important Note

Rejection of planned leave does not mean the employee is being denied legally protected leave. Medical leave, pregnancy-related leave, disability accommodation, FMLA, and other protected situations should be reviewed by HR.

## 8.4 Emergency Leave Procedure

Emergency Leave applies when an employee cannot provide normal advance notice due to an unexpected situation.

Examples may include:

* Sudden illness
* Family emergency
* Accident
* Death in the family
* Home emergency
* Urgent childcare issue
* Transportation emergency
* Safety-related issue

### Steps

1. Notify your manager as soon as safely possible.
2. Use the fastest practical communication method.
3. Provide a brief explanation and expected duration of absence.
4. If you cannot reach your manager, contact HR at [hr@roadmapconsulting.com](mailto:hr@roadmapconsulting.com) or ext. 3030.
5. Submit the leave request in [https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com](https://leaves.roadmapconsulting.com) as soon as you are able.
6. Select the most appropriate leave type.
7. Add a note that the request is emergency-related.
8. Upload documentation if required.
9. Keep your manager updated if your return date changes.
10. Contact HR if the emergency may require extended leave.

Managers are responsible for applying the leave policy fairly and consistently.

## Managers Must

* Consider business needs and employee circumstances.
* Avoid unnecessary delays.

* Coordinate team coverage.
* Escalate policy questions to HR.
